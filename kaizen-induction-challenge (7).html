1Puzzle 1 · Warm-Up

Pick the Cheapest Route

A product travels: Supplier → Factory → Warehouse → Shop. There are 3 possible routes it can take. Each route lists what it costs at every step. Just add up each route and click the one with the lowest total.

2Puzzle 2 · Which Bucket?

Sort the Product

Businesses often sort their products into 4 simple buckets based on two things: how well it's selling right now, and how fast that market is growing. Here's a product that sells really well, but the market around it has stopped growing — it's steady, reliable, and doesn't need extra investment. Which bucket fits best?

2Puzzle 2 · Growth Move

Pick the Move

If a business wants to grow, it can either sell something new or sell somewhere new. Here's the scenario: the club keeps the same event/product it already runs, but launches it at a college it has never reached before. What kind of growth move is that?

3Puzzle 3 · Word Play

Unscramble the Words

4 jumbled-up words below. All 4 are words you'll hear a lot in the club — use the hint if you're stuck. Type your answer in each box, capital or small letters both work.

4Puzzle 4 · Think It Through

Who Earns More?

Shop A makes ₹50 profit per item, but sells slowly.
Shop B makes only ₹20 profit per item, but sells much faster and in bigger volumes.
Which shop is definitely earning more overall?

4Puzzle 4 · Pricing Sense

Price It Right

A canteen sells samosas for ₹20 and sells 100 a day. They're thinking of dropping the price to sell more. If they price it too low, they lose money per samosa even if they sell a lot more. If they price it too high, hardly anyone buys. What's the smartest way to find the best price?

5Puzzle 5 · Final Challenge

Crack the Code

Each icon stands for a number. Use the first 3 lines to figure out the value of each icon — then use those values to solve the last line. Remember: multiply before you add!
